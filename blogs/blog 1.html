How to read properly?

Reading has become the most crucial aspect of the learning process, intelligence, and emotional side of humanity but I'd say we are doing something wrong while reading.
Have you ever thought about the posture position, lighting, and speed of reading?
Probably no. So why not find out?

3 min read

We read all the time but we don't even consider the environment that we are in whilst doing it. Our goal is simply to finish the book and not consider opening it again.
While reading there are three main requirements we should meet namely, posture position,environmental adjustments, and speed of reading.

Posture Positon

We tend to be bored and feel tired even though we've just started reading 15 minutes ago. There is a reason for that and it is posture. You may wonder what it has to do with feeling tired. Let me explain. First, one should find the best spot to read. For example, comfortable and bright enough. After that, sit properly and keep your head upwards. Most people keep their heads down or put the book down on a desk and bend their heads to read. These positions are the cause of feeling tired or even exhausted while studying, reading, etc. To position our heads properly we should lift the book to a point that our neck is straight. This will improve the blood flow through the brain and it will make us more focused.

Environmental Adjustments

After fixing our position we should also consider the light in the room we are in. When the brightness is dim we have a hard time seeing the letters and our eyes become fatigued resulting in inconvenient experiences. Seek glowing light or sunshine to get a better brightness and reading improves instantly. Additionally, the color of the paper has an impact on our eyes and our preferred color would be light yellow.

Somebody who reads a book and doesn't apply it, they're at no advantage over someone who's illiterate. - Jim Kwik

We covered posture, and brightness of the environment but we need to discuss the key method to comprehend the book. The methods discussed above are critical to reading with satisfaction but not understanding what we are reading will not get us far.

Speed Reading

There is a misconception that, if I read slowly, I will understand the book but sometimes slow reading is the cause of not grasping the information fully due to the lack of focus. When reading slowly our brain tries to think of different things and it will distract us from concentrating on the book. As a result, we don't know what we are reading and we reread the page again and again. This is not an ideal way of reading.
A solution would, as you already guessed, be speed reading. Try to read faster than you normally read. You will recognize the way your brain functions by trying to focus more on the book without unexpected thoughts. You will focus more and analyze what you are reading. For the first time, it will be uncomfortable because your brain is not used to reading fast so bear with it and keep practicing to develop the speed reading.

Conclusion

Most people don't read. According to the feedback they give us we can identify that the main cause of it is an unpleasant experience they get while doing reading. They feel tired right off the bat and not comprehending what they just read makes them give up reading. With the help of three simple methods, we can overcome these issues. Methods are, straight neck posture, enough light shine to the book, and finally reading fast/faster than usual.
Doing so people will get a chance to fully enjoy the book they were planning to read for months and excitement of reading will emerge through their eyes.
